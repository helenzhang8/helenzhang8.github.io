I worked in a group to create a website for the startup Hotplate. Without visiting the startup's website, we sketched out design ideas, made wireframes, and created hi-fi prototypes. We then finalized our prototypes after receiving feedback from an industry professional and conducted user testing.

Hotplate allows customers to order food from local chefs, as well as provide chefs with the platform and tools to sell their food. This startup aims to connect two needs: work for chefs out of business due to the pandemic and home-cooked meals for customers who are too busy to cook for themselves or would like to try something new. We decided to create a web interface.

Critique


Home
  • Even though "Browse home restaurants near you" is underlined, it is not interactive
FAQ
  • Too much information
  • Low color contrast (salmon and white) that can make it difficult for those who are visually impaired.
About
  • Missing button to submit "Contact Us" form
Browse
  • Search bar should not only be located at the bottom
  • Drop shadows are inconsistent and perhaps unnecessary when flat UI is more accessible
Restaurant Profile
  • Cart is not accessible - only visible from a specific restaurant's profile
  • Back button does not function

Brief Summary of Changes:

  • Fixed all links, added missing components
  • Added back button in cart
  • Changed FAQ page layout and color scheme for greater visibility
  • Added cart button to browse page
  • Created more consistent drop shadow styles on browse page to denote clickability
  • Scroll-locked prototype when viewing the popup for wontons in chili oil
  • Made text clickable on home page and made it take you to browse page


Testing



Results




Post-Test Questions & Analysis:

  • Restaurant did not have a "menu" page- not sure if there were any other menu options
  • Prototype was not searchable - ie initial browse page.
  • Wanted more clarity on delivery and pickup options
  • Multiple users did not realize the website could scroll
  • Interface was very familiar, and process was very streamlined.

Takeaway:

  • Preferable to test a real website- users expect functionality
  • Explore possible options e.g. delivery, menu choices to make the prototype more realistic
  • Perhaps refine options to get from the Home page to the Browse page
  • Streamline information delivery and consider adjusting some of the page contents



Overall Takeaway


This assignment was a good opportunity to work on a group design project. The startup we chose was really interesting- I think it fulfills a need we've experienced many times during the pandemic.

I'm also grateful that we had the opportunity to receive feedback from an expert in the field, as well as potential users. Everyone provided really valuable feedback, and I'm appreciative of the time they took to visit out website.

We sent our final prototype to Hotplate as the final part of our project. We hope that they enjoy seeing it, if not for the design, for the knowledge that we were inspired by their idea.
