hotsauce.com



I completed a Responsive Redesign for hotsauce.com- this involved analyzing the website to identify usability problems, creating low-fidelity and high-fidelity prototypes, and building a responsive website.

I encountered this website when shopping for my best friend's birthday gift. She has a notoriously high heat tolerance, so I thought I'd buy her some hot sauce. I searched for "hot sauce" on Google and selected the first option. I was immediately overwhelmed.

Visit hotsauce.com


Website


hotsauce.com has the benefit of an extremely recognizable domain name, as well as being the first search result. However, its website design is extremely outdated. This may be an intentional choice on the part of hotsauce.com- more questionable design could be equated to lower prices. Hotsauce.com comes off as a wholesale seller with reduced prices, if not somewhat suspicious.

However, its competitors generally have more "palatable" designs.

Usability Problems



Overall Design

  • Website is very overwhelming
  • Looks a bit sketchy and outdated
  • Too many different colors
    • Red and green contrast too much, assigned in an alternating pattern.
    • Not enough contrast between red fonts and orange background
    • Gradients look outdated
  • Font is skinny, making it difficult to read
  • General lack of consistency

Responsiveness

  • When shrinking the screen, it becomes distorted- especially heading
  • Past a certain point drop-down menu appears, but menu and button are separated by heading
  • When zooming out, heading background repeats

1. Heading Links

  • Too much information
  • Layout doesn't make sense- choice of links for left and right is confusing
  • Home link is redundant- logo can serve as link
  • Some links would be better off as categories or subcategories
  • Many links are redundant- already represented as categories

2. Top Banner

  • Not prominent enough- line underneath is very thin
  • Text could be more succint

3. Logo

  • Too complicated, has too many colors
  • Name is hard to read, lacks contrast
  • Looks outdated
  • Slogan doesn't make sense
  • Fonts don't match

4. Categories

  • Category selection is a bit random
  • Drop down subcategories could be helpful
  • Titles should be standardized e.g. plurals, punctuation
  • "Hottest Daily Deals" is a bit gimmicky

5. Banner

  • Inconsistent picture dimensions
  • Low quality images
  • Selector would be nice
  • Image choice is strange

6. Additional Categories

  • More complicated
  • Redundancies with primary categories
  • Nice idea to have picture categories though

7. Featured Products

  • Brands listed twice- in name and underneath
  • Poorly responsive- too many in a row, 5 stars usually don't fit in one line
  • All prices reduced, but only some "on sale"
  • View Product buttons don't line up

8. Hottest New Products

  • Redundant
  • Scroll bar is nice

9. Brands

  • Some brands do not have products on site
  • No apparent order

10. Mailing List

  • Subscribe button is on the top

11. Footer

  • Redundant
  • Doesn't appear the most trustworthy
  • Has potential!

Accessibility


  • Using the WAVE tool showed that the website is missing alternative text, headings, and heading structure in many places.

  • A lot of areas on the page have very low contrast between colors, making them hard to read for certain users. This is especially obvious on low saturation.

  • The random color changes are also quite hard to understand and follow.

  • Using a Screen Reader showed that the large amount of text at the top of the screen was very hard to understand and navigate. A lot of this content would be better suited for the bottom of the page.

  • Obstructive pop-up ads are not user-friendly.

Low-Fidelity Prototypes


Addressing Usability Issues:

  • Change color scheme and font choice
  • Create new top announcement bar with greater visibility and less test
  • Reorganize categories so they make sense and there is less heading text- this will be reflected in the dropdown menus on the real website
  • Curate brands and move brand carousel higher up on page
  • Changed product presentation to make it more uniform
  • Eliminated Hottest Products section
  • Moved Subscribe button for e-mail form
  • More official footer
  • Handled responsiveness issues


High-Fidelity Prototypes


Visual Design Approach:

  • Tried to be a bit flashy/wacky to retain the original feeling of the website
  • Established a thematic color scheme
  • Contrasting colors for text visibility, while remaining within scheme
  • Darkest colors at top and bottom (slight gradient), white background for products
  • Different backgrounds for each category
  • Fonts- one accent font for title, sans serif font for other headings and text
  • Simplified logo
  • Brand PNGs for clean look, brand recognition and consumer trust
  • Higher quality images, uniformity within categories
  • Responsiveness to reduce content on page, eliminate clutter

Overall Takeaway


Responsive design turned out to be quite difficult. To make the website truly responsive requires much more skill than I currently have. I used Bootstrap for certain aspects of this website, but experienced difficulty in others- plain Flexbox usually worked for these situations. However, there are still spots on the page that are a bit finnicky.

Overall, the website accommodates the three sizes as well as different font sizes. It is also fairly interactive! Feel free to check it out :)
